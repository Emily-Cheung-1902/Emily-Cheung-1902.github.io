Journal Articles

[1] Zhang, Y., & Ma, Z. (2025). Depression trajectories from mid to late life (50–89 years): The roles of cohort, multimorbidity status, and national contexts across nine European countries. Social Science & Medicine, 385, 118605. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.socscimed.2025.118605

Using eight waves of SHARE data from adults aged 50–89 across nine European countries, this study applies hierarchical age–period–cohort growth-curve models to disentangle age, period, and cohort patterns in depressive symptoms, with bounding analysis used to assess the robustness of the APC findings. Depression follows a U-shaped age trajectory, declining from age 50 to around age 65 before increasing sharply after age 70. Later-born cohorts report higher levels of midlife depression, while period effects are near zero overall, except for greater volatility in Spain. Multimorbidity is associated with higher levels of depressive symptoms in later life, although its relationship with depression varies considerably across countries.
